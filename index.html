Research notes and future value-add ideas

The current Let Me Google That page is a JavaScript-driven static experience. Its public HTML exposes a classic search box, two buttons, generated share URL, copy/preview/shorten controls, share buttons, and a query-string player.

The main player reads ?q=..., animates a fake cursor to the search field, types the query, moves to the search button, shows a punchline, then redirects to a search destination. A l=1 flag selects the lucky button.

This implementation borrows the interaction model, not the assets or code: generate link, animate query, teach the action, and send the viewer toward a useful destination.
